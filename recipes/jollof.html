JOLLOF RICE

DESCRIPTION

This West African favorite is bold, smoky, and richly flavored. The rice is simmered in a spicy tomato and pepper sauce until each grain is coated in bright red-orange color. It has layers of taste from onions, curry, thyme, and stock, giving it a deep savory kick. Traditionally, it’s served with fried plantain, grilled chicken, or beef. Jollof is often called a party rice because it’s so festive and irresistible.

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 5 large tomatoes (or canned tomato puree)
  • 3 red bell peppers
  • 2 onions (1 for blending, 1 sliced)
  • 2 Scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp curry powder, thyme, and seasoning cubes
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • Salt to taste

STEPS

  1. Blend tomatoes, peppers, and 1 onion until smooth
  2. Heat oil in a pot, add sliced onions, then fry tomato paste for 2 minutes
  3. Add blended mixture, curry, thyme, and seasoning. Cook until sauce thickens.
  4. Add chicken stock, stir well.
  5. Wash and add rice, mix to coat in sauce.
  6. Reduce heat, cover with foil/lid, and let it cook slowlu until rice is done.
  7. Fluff with a fork and serve with chicken or plantain
